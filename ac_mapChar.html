Map characteristics

Details

Map characteristics involves analyzing and understanding the specific attributes and context of the project. The characteristics of the project are, type of product, team size, application domain, etc. By thoroughly understanding these project characteristics, the team gains a clear picture of the project's overall context and the potential risks that may arise from it.

This activity enables the team to assess how the project's unique characteristics may impact the likelihood and severity of identified risks. It also helps in identifying any inherent vulnerabilities or limitations that may increase the project's exposure to risks. By mapping project characteristics, the team can align risk management strategies with the project's specific needs and constraints, allowing for targeted and effective risk management practices.

Guidelines

  • Identify Project Goals and Objectives: Begin by clearly defining the goals and objectives of the project. This involves understanding the desired outcomes, the problem the project aims to solve, and the value it intends to deliver to stakeholders. Agile projects often focus on delivering incremental value through iterative development, so ensure that the goals align with the principles of agility.
  • Assess Project Requirements and Scope: Analyze the project requirements and scope to determine their feasibility and suitability for an agile approach. Agile projects typically work with evolving requirements, so it's important to prioritize and break down the requirements into manageable user stories or tasks. Identify the key features, functionalities, and deliverables that will drive value early in the development process.
  • Define Agile Methodology: Once you have a clear understanding of the project goals and requirements, select and tailor an appropriate agile methodology and associated practices. Popular methodologies include Scrum, Kanban, and Extreme Programming (XP). Consider factors such as team size, project complexity, and customer involvement to determine the best fit. Define the key ceremonies, roles, artifacts, and practices that will be utilized in the project to promote collaboration, transparency, and iterative development.
